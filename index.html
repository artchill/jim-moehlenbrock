Meet the Author

Jim Moehlenbrock

Jim Moehlenbrock is a writer whose curiosity refuses to stay in one lane. Over the years his notebooks have filled with three very different kinds of writing: the day-by-day record of a budget traveler crossing Asia by rail and on foot, the small proverbs he gathered and sharpened during years in the classroom, and the math puzzles he designs to keep restless minds limber. What ties them together is a simple conviction — that learning should feel like an adventure rather than a chore.

Whether he is mapping a route through an unfamiliar city, distilling a hard-won lesson into a single memorable line, or building a puzzle that rewards patience over speed, Jim writes for readers who enjoy the work of thinking. His books ask you to slow down, look closely, and take pleasure in the moment a problem finally gives way.

Travel Journal

A Budget Traveler's Daily Journal of His Solo Adventures Through Asia

A candid, day-by-day account of one traveler's solo backpacking route across Asia — the cheap rooms, the long train rides, the missed connections, and the small kindnesses that make a shoestring journey unforgettable. Equal parts itinerary and travelogue, it's a companion for anyone dreaming of their own departure.
